Academic Background

I received my degree in Computer Science and Engineering in 1998 from the University of California Davis. I decided that I liked Davis enough to stay for a PhD working with Professor Ken Joy and Dr. Mark Duchaineau through the Student Employee Graduate Research Fellowship(SEGRF) at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. I finished my dissertation, Multiresolution Tetrahedral Meshes for Scientific Visualization, in September 2004.

I am now working at Stratovan Corporation, a startup in Davis CA developing visualization software for TSA and Medical applications. Previously I worked at Electronic Arts as a Software Engineer doing real-time rendering and visual effects. and Zynga doing backend development for social gaming network applications.

I created a blog, Deep and Shallow Thoughts with Ben,to keep track of interesting stuff I find on the internet.

Graduate Research

My research focused in the areas of scientific visualization for large volumetric datasets. It combines aspects of geometric modeling (i.e. view-dependent rendering of surfaces) and computer graphics (i.e. efficient shading techniques using texturing, vertex, and fragment programs).

I worked on multiresolution algorithms for exploring large datasets using recursive tetrahedral meshes. In this work we developed fast, flexible data structures and algorithms for the visualization of large volumetric datasets (i.e. 2Kx2Kx2K) on a variety of platforms from desktop machines to large shared memory multiprocessor machines.

My main research interests lie in interactive techniques for scientific visualization and geometric modeling. This includes multiresolution algorithms and techniques for isosurface extraction and volume rendering of large datasets, level-of-detail techniques for interactive, out-of-core, rendering of large polygonal surfaces and algorithms for geometric modeling including adaptive distance distance fields, subdivision surfaces and deformable models.
